Unit I

Concepts of Ecosystem (Q1–Q25)

🔥 Top Sure-Shot Topics – Unit I

  • Definition of Ecology – Ernst Haeckel (1866)
  • Tansley coined 'Ecosystem' (1935)
  • Lindeman's 10% Energy Law
  • Food chain types – Grazing vs Detritus
  • Biogeochemical cycles – Carbon, Nitrogen, Phosphorus
  • Population growth – J-curve vs S-curve (Logistic equation)
  • Ecological pyramids – Energy always upright
  • NPP = GPP – Respiration
Unit II

Ecological Features (Q26–Q50)

🔥 Top Sure-Shot Topics – Unit II

  • Inter-specific interactions – Mutualism, Commensalism, Parasitism, Amensalism
  • Pioneer & Climax community in succession
  • Fundamental vs Realized Niche (Hutchinson)
  • r vs K strategies (MacArthur & Wilson)
  • Gause's Competitive Exclusion Principle
  • Adaptations – Fossorial (Mole), Cursorial (Cheetah), Volant (Bat), Arboreal (Monkey)
Unit III

Environmental Pollution (Q51–Q75)

🔥 Top Sure-Shot Topics – Unit III

  • Greenhouse gases – CO₂, CH₄, N₂O, CFCs
  • Ozone depletion – Dobson unit, Montreal Protocol (1987)
  • Acid rain – pH < 5.6, SO₂ + NOx → H₂SO₄ + HNO₃
  • BOD & COD – water pollution indicators
  • Minamata disease (Mercury), Itai-Itai (Cadmium), Bhopal (MIC)
  • Biomagnification – DDT in food chain
  • Ecological footprint & Carbon footprint
Unit IV

Environmental Management (Q76–Q100)

🔥 Top Sure-Shot Topics – Unit IV

  • Environment Protection Act 1986 – India's umbrella legislation
  • National Green Tribunal (NGT) Act 2010 – Retired SC judge chairs
  • UNEP – Founded 1972, Stockholm Conference, HQ: Nairobi
  • Kyoto Protocol (1997), Paris Agreement (2015), Rio Summit (1992)
  • Sanitary Landfill – compacted & layered burial
  • Incineration (high temp burning), Composting (aerobic), Pyrolysis (no oxygen)
  • 3R – Reduce, Reuse, Recycle
